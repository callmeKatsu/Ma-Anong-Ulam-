Pork Menudo Recipes

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb (about 500g) pork, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 lb (about 115g) pork liver, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup water or beef broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Cooking oil for sauteing

Procedure:

  1. Cut the pork into bite-sized pieces and marinate in a mixture of soy sauce and minced garlic for at least 1 hour.
  2. In a large pot, combine the marinated pork (including the marinade), 1 cup of vinegar,
    1 cup of water, 1 tablespoon of peppercorns, and 2 bay leaves. Bring to a boil over high heat.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 30 minutes or until the pork is tender.
    Add more water if necessary to keep the pork submerged in the liquid.
  4. Season the dish with salt and optionally sugar, to taste.
  5. Continue to simmer until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  6. Serve the pork menudo over steamed rice and garnish with chopped scallions, if desired. Enjoy your meal!
