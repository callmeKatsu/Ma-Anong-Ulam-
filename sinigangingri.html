Sinigang Recipes

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly (ribs and belly), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium sized radish, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 medium sized green chili peppers (siling pansigang)
  • 1 medium sized onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium sized tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 packet sinigang mix (around 40 grams)
  • 1 bundle kangkong (water spinach), leaves separated from stems
  • 1 medium sized eggplant, sliced
  • 1 medium sized bunch of okra
  • 2 liters of water
  • Fish sauce (patis) or salt, to taste

Procedure:

  1. Clean and prepare the pork, making sure to cut it into 2-inch chunks.
    Rinse it thoroughly and place in a pot with 1.5 liters of water. Bring to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, lower the heat to medium and skim off the scum that rises to the surface.
    Add the chopped tomatoes, onions, garlic, and ginger. Cover the pot and let it simmer until
    the pork is tender and the flavors are well-combined.
  3. While the pork is cooking, prepare the vegetables.
    Cut the radish into chunks, and slice the okra, string beans, and kangkong stalks.
    Set aside the kangkong leaves for later.
  4. Once the pork is tender, add the sinigang mix and the radish chunks.
    Stir well and bring to a boil. Add the tamarind base and stir again.
  5. Add the sliced vegetables to the pot in the following order:
    first the okra and string beans, followed by the kangkong stalks, and finally the pepper and
    kangkong leaves. Cover the pot and let the vegetables cook in the soup for 5 minutes.
  6. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with fish sauce or salt, as needed.
    Serve hot with rice and bagoong or patis (fish sauce) on the side.
