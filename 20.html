Pinoy Steak with Onion ring Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork steak, about 1/2 inch thick
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Onion rings for serving
  • Chopped parsley or spring onions for garnish

Procedure:

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, sugar, and black pepper.
    Marinate the pork steak in the mixture for at least 1 hour.
  2. In separate shallow bowls, place the flour, breadcrumbs, and beaten egg.
  3. Remove the pork steak from the marinade, allowing the excess to drip off.
    Dredge the pork steak in flour, dip it into the beaten egg, and coat it with breadcrumbs.
  4. In a frying pan, heat oil over medium-high heat. Fry the breaded pork steak for about 3-4 minutes per side,
    until golden brown and cooked through. Remove from the pan and drain on paper towels.
  5. Season the fried pork steak with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the pork steak with onion rings on the side. Garnish with chopped parsley or spring onions.
